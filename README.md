This dashboard is an HR Analytics / Employee Attrition Analysis Dashboard.

What the Data Is About

The data contains information about employees in a company, such as:

Employee demographics (Age, Gender)
Department (R&D, Sales, HR)
Job Roles
Education Field
Job Satisfaction Ratings
Employee Attrition (whether an employee left the company)

The main purpose of this dashboard is to help HR understand:

How many employees are leaving the company
Which departments have the highest attrition
Which age groups are more likely to leave
Whether gender, education, or job satisfaction influence attrition.

**Key Insights**
1. Overall Attrition Rate is 16.12%
Total Employees: 1,470
Employees Left (Attrition): 237
Active Employees: 1,233
This means approximately 1 out of every 6 employees left the organization, indicating a moderate attrition level.
2. R&D Department Has the Highest Attrition
R&D: 133 employees (56.12%)
Sales: 92 employees (38.82%)
HR: 12 employees (5.06%)
More than half of the total attrition comes from the R&D department, making it the primary area of concern.
3. Employees Aged 25–34 Have the Highest Turnover
The largest employee population is in the 25–34 age group.
Attrition by age-group chart shows this group contributes the highest number of exits (112 employees).
This suggests younger and early-career employees are more likely to leave the company.
4. Life Sciences and Medical Background Employees Leave More Often
Attrition by Education Field:
Life Sciences: 89
Medical: 63
Marketing: 35
Technical Degree: 32
Employees from Life Sciences and Medical education backgrounds account for the majority of attrition, indicating possible retention challenges in these talent pools.
